Beginner's Guide FAQ

Q1: What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is a short, intensive event where people collaborate to build projects, often over 24–48 hours.

Q2: Do I need to be an expert programmer?

No! Hackathons welcome all skill levels. Beginners can learn a lot by participating and collaborating.

Q3: How do I find or form a team?

You can join with friends or find teammates at the event or through online communities set up by the organizers.

Q4: What should I bring to a hackathon?

Bring your laptop, charger, necessary tools, snacks, water, and an open mind ready to learn and collaborate.

Q5: How do I come up with a project idea?

Ideas can come from problems you care about, event themes, or just building something fun and interesting.

Q6: What if I don’t finish my project?

That’s okay! Hackathons are about learning and experimentation. Finishing is great, but growth matters more.

Q7: Which programming languages or tools should I use?

Use what you’re comfortable with or want to explore. Most hackathons support a wide range of tech stacks.

Q8: How are projects judged?

Judges evaluate creativity, technical complexity, usefulness, and presentation. Prizes may be involved too.

Q9: Can I participate remotely?

Yes! Many hackathons now offer online participation options. Always check event-specific details.

Q10: How should I prepare before the hackathon?

Brush up your coding, install needed tools, brainstorm ideas, and get good sleep before the event begins!
